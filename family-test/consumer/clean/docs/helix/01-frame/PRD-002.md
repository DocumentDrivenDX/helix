# Mobile Subscriber Onboarding PRD

## Summary

Create a mobile onboarding flow for new paid subscribers that reduces
first-week churn by helping each subscriber reach a meaningful product outcome
during the first session and return within seven days. The flow will replace a
generic post-purchase landing experience with a short, adaptive path that
captures subscriber intent, explains the most relevant paid benefit, routes the
subscriber to one recommended first action, and triggers lifecycle follow-up
when the subscriber stalls.

The first release targets subscribers who start or resume a paid plan from iOS
or Android. The product should reduce first-week churn by 10%, increase
first-session core-action completion by 15%, and improve day-7 subscriber
retention by 5% without reducing purchase conversion, blocking paid
entitlements, or increasing support contacts.

## Problem and Goals

### Problem

New subscribers often pay before they have experienced the product's strongest
value for their own goal. After purchase, they land in the broad mobile app
experience with limited guidance about what to do first, which paid benefit
matters, or how to turn the subscription into an immediate result. This creates
a gap between purchase intent and first successful usage.

First-week churn is concentrated among subscribers who do not complete a core
action, do not personalize the product, or do not return after the first
session. The current experience depends on subscribers discovering the right
path themselves, which makes early value inconsistent and leaves lifecycle
teams with limited context for targeted follow-up.

### Goals

1. New subscribers can understand and complete the next best action for their
   goal during the first paid session.
2. Product and lifecycle teams can personalize first-week follow-up based on
   onboarding state and first action completion.
3. The app can measure the subscriber onboarding funnel end to end so churn
   drivers are visible by platform, plan, subscriber segment, and goal.
4. Subscribers who skip or abandon onboarding still retain immediate access to
   paid features.

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Measurement Method |
|--------|--------|--------------------|
| First-week churn | Reduce by 10% among eligible new mobile subscribers versus the pre-launch baseline | Subscription analytics cohort comparing eligible subscribers in the experiment to control |
| First-session core-action completion | Increase by 15% among eligible subscribers | Product analytics event `subscriber_first_core_action_completed` within the first paid session |
| Day-7 subscriber retention | Increase by 5% without reducing purchase conversion | Retention dashboard for day-7 active paid subscribers, segmented by onboarding exposure |
| Onboarding completion | At least 70% of subscribers who enter onboarding complete it | Onboarding funnel events from eligibility through completion |
| Onboarding abandonment | No more than 15% of subscribers abandon before completion | Funnel drop-off between first onboarding screen and terminal state |
| Support contact rate | At or below current baseline for onboarding, entitlement confusion, and account access | Support tags per 1,000 eligible subscribers |

### Non-Goals

- Redesigning the subscription purchase flow, paywall, pricing, trial policy,
  or plan packaging.
- Adding new paid features or changing entitlement rules.
- Building a full preference center beyond intent capture needed for onboarding
  personalization.
- Creating desktop, web, tablet-specific, or customer-success-led onboarding.
- Building cancellation, churn-save, or win-back flows for subscribers who have
  already initiated cancellation.
- Replacing lifecycle marketing systems or analytics platforms.

## Users and Scope

### Primary Persona: Newly Paid Mobile Subscriber

**Role**: Individual subscriber who has just purchased a paid plan on iOS or
Android.

**Goals**: Confirm the subscription was worth paying for, understand what to do
first, and accomplish a useful outcome quickly.

**Pain Points**: The default app surface is broad, paid benefits are not tied
to the subscriber's goal, and the subscriber may leave before completing the
first meaningful action.

### Secondary Persona: Upgraded Free User

**Role**: Existing free user who upgrades on mobile after encountering a paid
feature, paywall, or trial conversion moment.

**Goals**: Understand what changed after upgrade, continue the task that led to
purchase, and discover the most relevant paid capability without repeating
basic setup.

**Pain Points**: Generic onboarding can feel redundant, while missing guidance
can leave the user unaware of subscription value.

### Secondary Persona: Lifecycle Product Manager

**Role**: Product owner responsible for activation, retention, and churn in
the subscriber lifecycle.

**Goals**: Measure onboarding impact, identify first-week drop-off drivers,
and test onboarding variants without requiring a new mobile app release for
content-only changes.

**Pain Points**: Current early-retention dashboards do not reliably connect
purchase intent, onboarding behavior, first action completion, and churn.

## Requirements

### Must Have (P0)

1. Eligible new mobile subscribers are routed into onboarding after purchase or
   first paid-session launch.
2. The flow captures subscriber intent or allows an explicit skip.
3. The flow recommends one goal-relevant first action and makes it possible to
   complete or launch that action.
4. Onboarding state is persisted and resumable across app backgrounding,
   crashes, retries, and device changes.
5. Paid entitlements remain accessible even if onboarding, analytics, remote
   configuration, or lifecycle systems are unavailable.
6. The funnel emits stable analytics events for eligibility, progress,
   completion, skips, first action completion, and first-week churn analysis.

### Should Have (P1)

1. The flow adapts copy and recommended actions by plan tier, selected goal,
   prior account history, and platform.
2. Lifecycle messaging is triggered from onboarding state for subscribers who
   skip, abandon, or complete without a first core action.
3. Content ordering, copy, goal options, and recommended first actions can be
   adjusted through remote configuration with rollback.
4. Win-back subscribers receive a shorter path that acknowledges prior usage
   when account history supports it.

### Nice to Have (P2)

1. The product can test multiple recommended actions for the same goal.
2. The flow can show lightweight proof points such as saved time, unlocked
   limits, or examples from similar subscribers.
3. Support tooling can display the subscriber's onboarding terminal state.

## Functional Requirements

### Subsystem: Eligibility and Entry

- **FR-1** - Detect eligible subscribers after mobile purchase completion,
  subscription restoration, or first paid-session app launch.
- **FR-2** - Route eligible subscribers to onboarding before the default home
  experience unless they have completed, skipped, or been excluded from the
  flow.
- **FR-3** - Exclude subscribers who are not newly paid, are outside the
  experiment audience, are unsupported by platform version, or have a resolved
  onboarding terminal state.
- **FR-4** - Allow subscribers to exit or skip onboarding and reach the app
  home without losing access to paid features.

### Subsystem: Intent Capture and Personalization

- **FR-5** - Present a concise set of primary goal options with plain-language
  labels and a skip option.
- **FR-6** - Persist the selected goal and use it to determine onboarding copy,
  paid-benefit messaging, and the recommended first action.
- **FR-7** - Personalize the path using selected goal, plan tier, platform,
  prior account history, and available feature entitlements.
- **FR-8** - Avoid asking for sensitive personal information unless already
  approved for product personalization and covered by privacy disclosures.

### Subsystem: First Action Guidance

- **FR-9** - Present exactly one recommended first action as the primary call
  to action for the selected path.
- **FR-10** - Let the subscriber complete the recommended first action inside
  onboarding or continue through a deep link that opens the correct product
  surface immediately after onboarding.
- **FR-11** - Highlight the paid benefit most relevant to the selected goal and
  plan tier without upselling features already included in the subscription.
- **FR-12** - Record whether the subscriber completed the recommended first
  action during the first paid session.

### Subsystem: State, Resilience, and Recovery

- **FR-13** - Save onboarding progress locally and server-side so subscribers
  can resume after app backgrounding, crashes, retries, offline use, or device
  changes.
- **FR-14** - Treat onboarding state writes as idempotent so duplicate launches,
  retries, and delayed network responses do not create conflicting terminal
  states.
- **FR-15** - Degrade to the app home with paid entitlements intact when
  onboarding services, analytics, remote configuration, or lifecycle messaging
  fail.
- **FR-16** - Support rollback of remote onboarding configuration within 15
  minutes.

### Subsystem: Lifecycle Follow-Up and Analytics

- **FR-17** - Emit analytics events for eligibility, entry, screen view, goal
  selection, skip, abandonment, completion, first action start, first action
  completion, reminder trigger, and first-week churn attribution.
- **FR-18** - Trigger first-week lifecycle follow-up based on onboarding state:
  completed, skipped, abandoned, or completed without first core action.
- **FR-19** - Segment funnel and churn reporting by platform, plan tier,
  subscriber type, selected goal, experiment variant, and terminal state.
- **FR-20** - Provide experiment hooks for ordering, copy, goal options, and
  recommended actions without a mobile release when remote configuration
  supports the change.

## Non Functional Requirements

- **NFR-1** - The first onboarding screen must render within 300ms median added
  latency after subscriber eligibility is known.
- **NFR-2** - The flow must support current mobile accessibility requirements,
  including small screens, large text, screen readers, reduced motion, color
  contrast, and dark mode.
- **NFR-3** - Onboarding state persistence must tolerate offline-to-online
  transitions, duplicate requests, app restarts, and delayed server responses.
- **NFR-4** - No onboarding dependency may block entitlement access or account
  sign-in.
- **NFR-5** - Analytics schemas must be documented, versioned, and compatible
  with existing subscription, retention, and churn dashboards.
- **NFR-6** - Remote configuration changes must be auditable and reversible
  within 15 minutes.
- **NFR-7** - The flow must avoid collecting new sensitive personal information
  unless privacy review approves the collection and disclosure.

## Technical Context

- **Mobile Platforms**: iOS and Android supported production app versions.
- **Account and Subscription State**: Existing authentication, subscription,
  purchase, restore, and entitlement services govern eligibility.
- **Configuration**: Existing remote configuration or experimentation platform
  controls audience, copy, ordering, goal options, and recommended actions.
- **Analytics**: Existing product analytics and subscription data warehouse
  must receive versioned onboarding events.
- **Lifecycle Messaging**: Existing push, email, or in-app messaging system
  sends first-week follow-up based on onboarding state.
- **Data/Storage**: Client local storage plus server-side onboarding state keyed
  to account and subscription identity.
- **Platform Targets**: Mobile phones only for first release; tablet, desktop,
  and web are excluded.

## Constraints, Assumptions, Dependencies

### Constraints

- **Technical**: Onboarding cannot require a mobile release for content-only
  experiment changes once remote configuration is in place.
- **Technical**: Onboarding must not block the app home or paid entitlement
  access when dependent services fail.
- **Business**: The first release must focus on first-week subscriber churn, not
  broader long-term retention or cancellation-save journeys.
- **Legal/Compliance**: Personalization must use approved account, plan, and
  product-behavior data only.

### Assumptions

- Current analytics can identify eligible mobile subscribers, first-session
  behavior, first core action completion, and churn within seven days.
- Product has at least one known high-value first action per supported goal.
- Remote configuration or experimentation infrastructure can safely target
  eligible subscribers by app version, platform, plan, and subscriber segment.
- Lifecycle messaging systems can consume onboarding terminal state within the
  first week.
- Existing subscription entitlement systems are authoritative and do not need
  changes for this initiative.

### Dependencies

- Subscription and entitlement service for eligibility, plan tier, purchase
  restoration, and paid access checks.
- Mobile app teams for iOS and Android onboarding surfaces and deep links.
- Analytics engineering for event schema, dashboard updates, and churn cohort
  measurement.
- Lifecycle marketing for reminder content and delivery rules.
- Privacy or legal review for any personalization inputs beyond existing
  approved account and product-behavior data.
- Experimentation or remote configuration platform for rollout, A/B testing,
  and rollback.

## Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Onboarding adds friction and reduces paid conversion or first-session engagement | Medium | High | Start onboarding after purchase, keep skip visible, monitor conversion and session engagement in the experiment guardrail dashboard |
| Personalization recommends an irrelevant first action | Medium | High | Start with a small goal taxonomy, map each goal to validated actions, and review action completion by goal before expanding variants |
| Analytics gaps make churn impact inconclusive | Medium | High | Define event schema before implementation, validate events in pre-launch QA, and run an A/A or holdout check before full rollout |
| Dependency failure blocks paid access | Low | High | Implement fail-open behavior to app home and entitlement checks independent from onboarding state |
| Lifecycle follow-up feels noisy or redundant | Medium | Medium | Suppress reminders after first action completion, cap first-week touches, and measure unsubscribe, opt-out, and support contacts |
| Remote configuration introduces inconsistent client behavior | Medium | Medium | Version config payloads, validate required fields before activation, and maintain 15-minute rollback |
| Accessibility issues increase abandonment for assistive technology users | Low | High | Include accessibility acceptance checks for screen readers, large text, reduced motion, color contrast, and small screens before rollout |

## Open Questions

- Which existing event is the canonical first core action for each subscriber
  goal? This blocks final goal-to-action mapping; ask Product Analytics and the
  Activation PM.
- Which lifecycle channels are approved for each onboarding terminal state?
  This blocks reminder delivery rules; ask Lifecycle Marketing.
- What is the current first-week churn baseline for eligible mobile subscribers
  by platform and plan tier? This blocks final experiment sizing; ask
  Analytics Engineering.
- Does the existing remote configuration platform support all required targeting
  fields and rollback timing? This blocks no-release copy and ordering tests;
  ask Mobile Platform.
- Which personalization inputs require additional privacy review? This blocks
  use of prior account history beyond current product-behavior data; ask Legal
  or Privacy.

## Success Criteria

- Eligible new mobile subscribers can enter, complete, skip, abandon, and
  resume onboarding without losing paid entitlements.
- Each supported goal maps to one recommended first action and one relevant
  paid-benefit message.
- The onboarding funnel and first-week churn impact are measurable by platform,
  plan tier, subscriber type, selected goal, experiment variant, and terminal
  state.
- First-week churn among eligible new mobile subscribers decreases by at least
  10% against the agreed baseline after full rollout.
- First-session core-action completion increases by at least 15% among eligible
  subscribers.
- Day-7 subscriber retention increases by at least 5% without reducing purchase
  conversion or increasing support contact rate.
