# Mobile Subscriber Onboarding PRD

## Summary

Create a mobile onboarding flow for new paid subscribers that helps them reach
their first meaningful product outcome within the first session and return
within the first week. The flow should replace a generic welcome experience
with a focused, adaptive path that captures intent, recommends the first
high-value action, and reinforces the subscriber's decision to pay.

The first release targets subscribers who start a paid plan from iOS or
Android. Success is measured by lower first-week churn, stronger activation,
and higher day-7 retention without adding friction to account creation or
subscription purchase.

## Problem

New subscribers are paying before they have fully learned how the product fits
their needs. After purchase, they currently enter the same broad product
surface as every other user, with limited guidance about what to do first or
how paid features translate into immediate value. This creates a gap between
the subscriber's purchase intent and their first successful usage moment.

First-week churn is concentrated among subscribers who do not complete a core
action, do not personalize the product, or do not return after the first
session. The onboarding flow must reduce that early uncertainty by making the
next best step obvious, achievable, and relevant to the subscriber's goal.

## Users

- New paid subscribers who complete purchase on mobile and open the app for
  the first time as subscribers.
- Returning free users who upgrade on mobile and need to understand what their
  subscription unlocks.
- Win-back subscribers who resubscribe and may need a shorter path that
  acknowledges prior usage.
- Customer support, lifecycle marketing, and product analytics teams who need
  visibility into onboarding completion, drop-off, and churn drivers.

## Functional Requirements

- FR-1: Detect eligible new subscribers after purchase or first paid-session
  app launch and route them into subscriber onboarding before the default home
  experience.
- FR-2: Collect the subscriber's primary intent from a concise set of goal
  options, with an option to skip and continue.
- FR-3: Personalize the onboarding path based on the selected goal, prior
  account history, plan tier, and platform.
- FR-4: Present the subscriber with one recommended first action that can be
  completed inside the onboarding flow or from a deep link immediately after
  onboarding.
- FR-5: Highlight the paid benefit most relevant to the subscriber's selected
  goal, using plan-aware copy and no upsell messaging for features already
  included in the subscription.
- FR-6: Save onboarding progress locally and server-side so subscribers can
  resume after app backgrounding, crashes, or device changes.
- FR-7: Allow subscribers to skip onboarding and reach the app home without
  losing access to paid features.
- FR-8: Trigger a first-week lifecycle follow-up based on onboarding state:
  completed, skipped, abandoned, or completed without first core action.
- FR-9: Emit analytics events for eligibility, screen views, intent selection,
  skips, completion, first action completion, reminders, and first-week churn.
- FR-10: Provide experiment hooks so product teams can A/B test ordering,
  copy, goal options, and recommended first actions without a mobile app
  release when remote configuration supports it.

## Non Functional Requirements

- NFR-1: The onboarding flow must add no more than 300ms median latency before
  the first onboarding screen after subscriber eligibility is known.
- NFR-2: The flow must work on supported iOS and Android versions, including
  small screens, large text settings, dark mode, and screen readers.
- NFR-3: Onboarding state writes must be idempotent and resilient to duplicate
  app launches, retries, and offline-to-online transitions.
- NFR-4: The system must not block paid entitlement access if onboarding
  services, analytics, remote configuration, or lifecycle messaging are down.
- NFR-5: Event schemas must be stable, documented, and compatible with existing
  subscription, retention, and churn dashboards.
- NFR-6: The flow must avoid collecting sensitive personal information unless
  already approved for product personalization and privacy disclosures.
- NFR-7: Remote configuration changes must support rollback within 15 minutes
  without requiring a client release.

## Success Metrics

- Reduce first-week churn among eligible new mobile subscribers by 10% relative
  to the current baseline within four weeks of full rollout.
- Increase eligible subscribers completing at least one core action in the
  first session by 15%.
- Increase day-7 subscriber retention by 5% without reducing subscription
  purchase conversion.
- Achieve at least 70% onboarding completion among eligible subscribers who
  enter the flow.
- Keep onboarding skip rate below 20% and abandonment rate below 15%.
- Maintain support contact rate for onboarding, entitlement confusion, and
  account access at or below the current baseline.

## Out of Scope

- Redesigning the subscription purchase flow, paywall, pricing, or plan
  packaging.
- Building a full preference center beyond the intent capture needed for
  onboarding personalization.
- Desktop, web, or tablet-specific onboarding experiences.
- New paid features or changes to entitlement rules.
- Churn-save flows for subscribers who have already initiated cancellation.
- Manual customer success outreach or concierge onboarding.
